After reading Best practices for form design

Reading the article made me consider designing from the users' perspective once again. It is very interesting to think about how small features like multiple columns exhausts users when they fill out forms, and how the location of placeholder text could change users' experience with forms.

Another thing I found intriguing from the article was auto focusing the first input field. I vaguely remember seeing these highlights in my personal interactions with forms, and it is surprising how these small visual cues just pass right through my thoughts. I think it is incredible that it is possible to design something that is so minimal, yet so impactful. After reading about this section, I once again thought that visual cues are much stronger than verbal cues and that it is the designers' job to utilize visiual cues to navigate users.

When I first learned about creating forms, I was only nervous about creating something that I have never thought of creating. However, reading this article made me more enthusiastic about creating a form. I hope that I would be able to utilize information that I learned from this article into my future projects.
